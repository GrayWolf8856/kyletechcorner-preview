Plant Hub v1.0

An ESP32-C3 plant watering controller PCB — designed, verified, and sent to fab. MIT licensed: download it, build it, remix it.

What it does

One small board waters a plant and runs its lights. A socketed ESP32-C3 Super Mini switches two independent 5V LED strip channels (white + grow) through logic-level MOSFETs, drives a water pump through a plug-in 5V relay module, and reads an analog soil-moisture sensor on a WiFi-safe ADC pin. Everything lands on screw terminals — no flying wires, no breadboard.

Board85 × 60 mm, 2-layer, ground pour both sides
BrainESP32-C3 Super Mini (socketed & removable, USB-C reachable in place)
Outputs2× LED channels (RFP30N06LE MOSFETs), 1× pump via relay module
InputAnalog soil-moisture sensor, 3.3V, on ADC1 (works with WiFi on)
PowerSingle 5V input on a screw terminal
Firmware pinsIO6 = white, IO7 = pump (HIGH = on), IO10 = grow, IO0 = soil

Build log

  • July 2026 — Breadboard to PCB
    The circuit started life validated on a breadboard, then got turned into a real schematic and board in KiCad. The whole design is generated from a single netlist file, so the schematic and PCB can never disagree with each other.
  • July 2026 — Verification pays off
    A pre-order design review caught a board-killer: the ESP32 socket footprint had its two pin columns mirrored. Plugging in the module would have put 5V into a GPIO pin. Cross-checking the footprint against photos of the real module caught it before ordering — the fix, plus a pump-gate pulldown and a ground-pour repair, all went in before a single board was made. Lesson for first-time designers: verify footprints against the physical part, not your memory.
  • July 2026 — Sent to fab
    Final design passed ERC and DRC with zero errors and zero unconnected nets at JLCPCB's design rules. Boards and parts are ordered — assembly and firmware are next.
